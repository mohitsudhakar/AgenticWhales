General Heuristics and Greek Mapping
Rather than relying on continuous, cost-heavy stochastic calculus to manage options, the SOTA agents will apply established trading heuristics to the "Greeks" (the mathematical parameters defining option risk) [cite: 4, 5].
The system treats these parameters as deterministic rules. For instance, the LLM evaluates "Delta" as a practical heuristic representing the rough probability of an option expiring in-the-money [cite: 5, 6]. A call option with a 0.30 delta is heuristically treated by the LLM's reasoning engine as having a ~30% chance of success, allowing the agent to rapidly rank strikes [cite: 6]. Furthermore, the LLM can instantly calculate the "effective leverage" of a proposed derivative trade by querying the data from the option chain and applying standard elasticity formulas, ensuring the user's directional risk is mathematically contained before execution [cite: 6].
Causal Reasoning for Market Structure and Skew
To effectively hedge, a system must understand why the market is moving, specifically focusing on how institutional dealers are positioned. SOTA models have demonstrated an emergent capacity to analyze implied volatility (IV) skew and term structure through pure causal reasoning, completely independent of custom training data.
Empirical testing reveals that when standard LLMs are fed unbiased, obfuscated options data (such as raw gamma exposure values across strikes), they can accurately detect complex dealer hedging constraints with over 70% accuracy [cite: 7]. The LLM recognizes that when downside out-of-the-money (OTM) puts become significantly more expensive than at-the-money (ATM) calls, the resulting "left-tail volatility smirk" indicates aggressive institutional hedging and impending market turbulence [cite: 8]. This occurs because market makers (dealers) who sell protective put options to large institutions effectively adopt a "short gamma" position. As the market drops, these dealers are forced to aggressively short the underlying asset themselves to maintain a delta-neutral book, thereby accelerating the market decline. By leveraging this causal understanding, the Risk Management Agent can proactively execute protective hedges when the volatility skew signals fear, rather than reacting passively to price drops.

Retail Constraints: Brokerage API, Margin, and Regulatory Realities
To seamlessly deploy this advanced options architecture, the coding assistant must navigate strict regulatory and API boundaries. Retail users cannot execute these complex "Recipes" on basic cash accounts.
